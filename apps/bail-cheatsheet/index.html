        6. Counsel Checklist

        Before stepping up

        • Read the synopsis. Confirm what the Crown is alleging.
        • Confirm onus (use box 2 above).
        • Note any reverse-onus that triggers s. 515(6); plan to address all three grounds at s. 515(10).
        • Identify Indigenous status (Gladue / Ipeelee) and any cultural / community supports.
        • Identify mental-health / fitness flags; ask about medication and substance issues.
        • Get sureties' names + ages + occupations + relationship + financial position; line up backup sureties.
        • Verify residence, employment, school, treatment commitments.
        • Check eligibility for the local bail program (box 5).

        At the hearing

        • State accused's name and the charges.
        • Confirm onus on the record.
        • Open at rung 1 of the Antic ladder. Force Crown to show cause for each step up.
        • Address each detention ground separately; do not let Crown blur primary / secondary / tertiary.
        • If detention sought on tertiary: respond to St-Cloud factors expressly.
        • Cite Zora on every condition: must be reasonably necessary, no more onerous than required, with factual nexus.
        • Avoid cash deposits unless accused is non-resident or 200+ km from court (s. 515(2)(d)).
        • If surety required, confirm it is necessary (not merely useful) per Antic ¶67(g).

        After release / detention order

        • If released: review every condition with the accused; explain breach exposure under s. 145.
        • If detained: confirm s. 525 review timing — 90 days for indictable, 30 days for summary.
        • Note any s. 520 / s. 521 review options and ask client about timing.
        • Update file with the recognizance and conditions; calendar the next appearance.
